Waste Management activities

Plastic Ban

The Government of Tamil Nadu is launching the "People's Campaign against throwaway plastics" announced during the Budget session 2021-2022 on 03.09.2021. This campaign will engage stakeholders from various sectors in each District, supported by the Tamil Nadu Pollution Control Board and District Collectors. Strategies include impactful communication, effective monitoring, promoting sustainable alternatives, and collaborating with industries for large-scale production of eco-friendly alternatives.

Door to Door Garbage collection

In Tamil Nadu, the Municipal Administration Department and organizations collect household waste through door-to-door collection. This is a significant initiative for overall management, finance, and environmental studies.
Below are the collection and operation details:
Waste Collection and Door-to-Door Collection: In all "135 municipalities", Battery Operated Vehicles (BOVs), Light Commercial Vehicles (LCVs), and other equipment are used to facilitate waste collection. This includes waste segregation at the source, household-to-household collection, transportation, and processing.

Composting and Organic Waste Management

Composting and organic waste management in Tamil Nadu are governed by acts such as the Tamil Nadu Local Bodies Act, 1920, and regulations by the Tamil Nadu Pollution Control Board (TNPCB). The Solid Waste Management Rules, 2016, and the Tamil Nadu Municipal Solid Waste Rules, 2000, outline guidelines for waste handling. Additionally, the Tamil Nadu Organic Waste Management Policy, 2019, promotes decentralized composting and vermiculture. Composting and vermicomposting guidelines issued by TNPCB provide technical assistance. Capacity-building programs and awareness campaigns are conducted to encourage community participation. Overall, these initiatives aim to promote sustainable waste management practices and reduce environmental pollution.

Recycling Facilitates

In Tamil Nadu, recycling facilities, overseen by the Tamil Nadu Pollution Control Board (TNPCB), ensure compliance with environmental standards. Waste materials are collected, segregated, and processed based on type and quality. Advanced machinery and manual labor are utilized for sorting and cleaning, preparing materials for recycling. Baling or compacting may occur for efficient transportation to recycling plants or markets. Once transported, recyclables undergo reprocessing to create raw materials for manufacturing new products, closing the recycling loop. Additionally, public awareness campaigns and educational programs promote recycling practices, fostering sustainability. These actions collectively facilitate efficient recycling operations in Tamil Nadu, conserving resources and reducing environmental impact.

Awarness campaigns

Awareness campaigns and community engagement in Tamil Nadu promote sustainable waste management practices and environmental conservation. These initiatives involve public education, school programs, and community workshops to raise awareness about waste segregation and recycling. Door-to-door campaigns provide guidance on waste management, while street plays and cultural events creatively convey messages about environmental stewardship. Social media and online campaigns amplify the reach of awareness efforts. Partnerships with local businesses, NGOs, and government agencies enhance the effectiveness of these initiatives, empowering communities to take collective action for a cleaner environment.

NGO'S involvement

Solid waste management in India is addressed significantly by NGOs, who play pivotal roles in various aspects. They create awareness about waste segregation and storage, conduct door-to-door waste collection services, and provide employment opportunities to marginalized communities. NGOs also handle e-waste management, coastal waste management, and advocate for responsible waste practices. Additionally, they offer technical assistance for waste management practitioners, engage in advocacy campaigns, and implement waste-to-energy projects. One inspiring example is Amutha, a Green Friend in Tamil Nadu, who has contributed to waste management and community development for nine years with HiH India.
